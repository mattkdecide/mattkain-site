2. Upside Opportunities

6 positions
01

Strategy Lead — Continuation of Existing Funded Role

The role was funded. The individual changed. The funding should continue.
P1   HIGH IMPACT

The 2023 Master SOW includes an EVP Strategy function at 0.75 FTE. This role was funded under the previous incumbent. The current APAC Strategy Lead is stepping directly into that same contracted role — same function, same scope, same place in the SOW structure.

Position: There is no basis for this role to become unfunded simply because the individual changed. The rollover should carry forward the existing funded position at the contracted rate. This is a continuation of an existing arrangement, not a new ask.

The strategy function has, if anything, become more demanding since 2023: the account now spans two contracts, a new India Hub, APAC Expansion scope, Firefly, and AEP. Continuity of funding is the expected and appropriate outcome.

Data needed: Confirm contracted rate and FTE allocation for the EVP Strategy role in the 2023 Master SOW staffing tables to establish the rollover baseline.
02

Regional Lead — Overhead Allocation for Multi-Market Coordination

The account has grown materially since 2023. A full-time coordinator is now required.
P1   HIGH IMPACT

At the time of the 2023 Master SOW, the APAC account operated as a single contract across a defined set of markets. It now spans two separate SOWs, four markets, a newly established India Hub, APAC Expansion scope, and multi-product complexity across Firefly, AEP, and Creative Cloud. The Regional Lead function is a full-time, multi-market coordination role not referenced in either current contract.

Position: Introduce partial funding via a pro-rata overhead allocation across markets, consistent with standard practice for retained multi-market accounts at this scale. Proposed allocation: 50% ANZ / 20% Korea / 15% Singapore-SEA / 15% India.
Data needed: Confirm applicable rate card for the Regional Lead function. Confirm prior renewal cycle history — was this raised before, and what was Adobe's response?
03

Korea — Introduce Management and Coordination Fee

No management fee mechanism exists in the current contract.
P1   REVENUE

Korea is delivered via a local subcontracted partner agency. The 2023 Master SOW bills Adobe at Seoul rate card but contains no management fee schedule or markup provision governing Zenith's position on the subcontract. The absence of a documented margin mechanism on a market of this scale is a structural gap in the contract.

Position: Introduce an explicit management and coordination fee for the Korea subcontract in the rolled-over agreement. Industry standard for subcontracted delivery at this scale is 12–15% on the annual pass-through value. The exact ask depends on the current subcontractor margin.
Data needed: Pull Korea subcontractor P&L from Finance before tabling this position. The direction of the ask — and whether it is framed as a new fee or a margin formalisation — depends entirely on the current margin. Do not proceed without this data.
04

India Hub — Rate Uplifts on Confirmed In-Seat Roles

Agreed rates should apply to roles that are active.
P1   REVENUE

The FY2026 SOW contains 5–6 TBH (to-be-hired) roles, several of which are now filled or in the process of being filled. A revised fee schedule prepared in April 2026 proposed rate increases across a number of India Hub roles, reflecting both market movement and role evolution since the original SOW was signed.

Position: For any role now confirmed in-seat and performing, the updated rate should apply from the rollover start date. This is not a speculative ask — it is application of agreed-in-principle rates to roles that are now active.
Data needed: Confirm current India Hub staffing actuals — which TBH roles are filled, in-seat, and from what date. Cross-reference against the April 2026 revised fee schedule to identify which rate uplifts are immediately applicable.
05

Flexible Resource Reserve — Recovery Vehicle for Ad Hoc Demand

Solves Adobe's stated requirement. Creates a fee recovery mechanism as a structural consequence.
P1   STRUCTURAL

Adobe's stated operational requirement is genuine flexibility — the ability to redeploy budget across markets, products, or channels without triggering a formal SOW amendment. The current SOW structure, with fixed service categories and planning caps, is structurally incompatible with this requirement.

Position: Introduce a Flexible Resource Reserve clause in the rolled-over agreement. A defined percentage of total APAC fee (indicatively 8–10%) becomes redeployable across markets, channels, or functions within defined parameters, subject to quarterly reconciliation. This directly addresses Adobe's stated requirement and simultaneously creates a documented recovery mechanism for ad hoc work that currently proceeds without fee capture.

This is a strong P1 position because it is framed as solving a problem for Adobe, not as an additional cost. The fee recovery benefit to Zenith is a structural consequence, not the stated rationale.

06

India — Scope Consolidation and Legacy Rate Retirement

Three fee structures, potential overlap risk, one market.
P2   STRUCTURAL

India currently carries three separate fee structures built at different points in time: the 2023 Master SOW India Ad Ops line (~8 FTEs at $17.19/hr), the FY2026 India Hub (15-role team at Hub rate card), and a Local Integrated Team whose contractual home is unconfirmed. The legacy $17.19/hr rate is materially below the India Hub rate card, and potential functional overlap between the two structures represents a compliance risk.

Position: Consolidate India under a single contract structure at the rollover. Retire the legacy $17.19/hr Ad Ops line and absorb those hours and functions into the India Hub SOW at current Hub rates. This removes the overlap risk, simplifies contract governance, and produces a modest net fee uplift on re-priced roles.
Data needed: Confirm the contractual home of the India Local Integrated Team before consolidating. Confirm whether legacy Ad Ops roles and Hub roles are performing distinct or overlapping functions.

3. Proposed Fee Reallocation

3 items
The items below do not represent new revenue to the account. They are structural adjustments to how existing contracted fees are allocated or recovered. Progress these in parallel with — not in place of — the P1 upside positions above.

H1 FY2026 Proration Reconciliation

Risk management
Current State
The FY2026 SOW contains proration provisions — TBH roles unfilled beyond defined periods attract fee adjustments. H1 reconciliation has not been completed.
Proposed Change
Run the H1 reconciliation internally before Adobe does. If credits are owed, quantify and manage proactively. Proactive disclosure on an unavoidable adjustment builds credibility for the P1 upside positions.
Action Required
Finance / India Hub account team to run H1 fee reconciliation. Do not enter the renewal conversation without this number.

Singapore — Internal Margin Optimisation

Internal only — no client disclosure
Current State
Singapore is billed at APAC activation hub rate card. Internal fulfilment model has evolved since the SOW was signed.
Proposed Change
Hold Singapore billing rates to Adobe at contracted levels. Review internal fulfillment model for efficiency gains. This is a Zenith internal margin action — not a client-facing conversation.
Action Required
Internal commercial review by APAC Client Lead and SG market lead. No client disclosure required or appropriate.

APAC Scope and Deliverables Annex

Structural protection
Current State
The 2023 Master SOW deferred all market-specific deliverables to post-signing onboarding sessions. Three years of delivery have proceeded without a formal contractual deliverables schedule.
Proposed Change
Attach a formal APAC Scope and Deliverables Schedule as an annex to the rolled-over SOW. Documents three years of actual delivery and protects both parties against future scope disputes.
Action Required
APAC Strategy Lead to draft scope annex from existing delivery records prior to the renewal conversation.
