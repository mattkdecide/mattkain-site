The Opportunity

A shared problem, a shared solution

The quality of creative assets is the single most leveraged variable in digital advertising performance. Poor creative degrades ad effectiveness, wastes media spend, and drives advertisers toward cheaper — but ultimately less capable — tools. The inverse is equally true: when SMBs and agencies produce better creative, everything upstream improves. Google Ads performance rises. Advertiser confidence and investment grow. Adobe's platform demonstrates measurable ROI. This is the foundation for a tri-party program where each party's commercial goals are served by the same outcome: more AU businesses producing better creative, more consistently.

Mutual Goals

What success looks like — for each party

Every program in this framework should be evaluated against all three sets of goals. Any initiative that serves only one party will not achieve the commercial commitment needed to sustain it.

Adobe

Differentiate Creative Cloud & Firefly. Drive MAU and ARR.

  • Grow Creative Cloud and GenStudio adoption among AU SMBs and independent agencies — a segment historically difficult to reach at scale
  • Demonstrate measurable performance lift from Adobe-produced creative versus generic AI alternatives
  • Position Firefly as the IP-safe, commercially indemnified standard for AI creative — ahead of Canva, free-tier competitors, and unbranded AI tools
  • Drive trial-to-paid conversion through contextually relevant partner distribution
  • Generate APAC case studies and data to support enterprise and agency sales motions globally
Google

Grow advertiser investment through better-performing campaigns.

  • Lift ad quality and creative effectiveness across the long-tail SMB and agency portfolio managed by the AU SMB/Agency team
  • Drive increased Google Ads spend from advertisers who see measurable ROAS improvement — higher confidence leads to higher budgets
  • Reduce campaign churn driven by poor creative performance and disengaged advertisers
  • Give the AU SMB/Agency team a differentiated capability to offer clients — beyond bid strategy and keyword optimisation
  • Strengthen agency partner relationships by equipping them with new accreditation and production capability
Publicis

Deepen the Adobe relationship and capture commercial upside in new formats.

  • Fulfil and extend its retained value to Adobe by acting as a strategic enabler of high-value partner programs Adobe cannot run alone
  • Establish a visible market position as the creative production and media partner of record for AU SMB transformation
  • Capture direct commercial upside in programs that sit outside the retained scope — principal media, production wraps, consulting mandates
  • Build a repeatable SMB services model that can be scaled across APAC markets
  • Generate category-defining thought leadership under the Publicis brand alongside Adobe and Google

Where to Start

Recommended entry points

Three programs designed to open the partnership without requiring large upfront commitment. Each stands alone, delivers visible results quickly, and opens the door to the larger triangulated plays once trust and commercial alignment are established.

1

A1 + A2 · Creative Certification Track

Certify Google AU's SMB/Agency field team (A1), then offer a micro-credential to their clients (A2). Low budget risk, high internal visibility. Gives the Google team a reason to champion Adobe internally without committing significant spend.

Low risk · High visibility · No budget commitment required to launch pilot
2

C3 · Campaign Fund with Creative Gate

The most direct and measurable way to drive Firefly adoption at volume. Google activates its existing campaign fund levers; Adobe gets tool adoption tied to real spend data. Clean attribution for both parties from day one.

Measurable · Uses Google's existing levers · Direct Firefly adoption signal
3

E2 · AI Creative Quality Index

Positions Adobe's IP-safety and quality story with empirical data. Gives Google a content asset for agency and advertiser conversations. Only requires research and marketing investment — no program infrastructure to stand up.

Marketing dollars only · Generates leads for all three parties · Builds the category narrative
Sequencing principle: All three entry points can run simultaneously and independently. They are designed to generate commercial credibility and internal champions before committing to the higher-investment programs in categories G, I, and J. Once trust is established and data exists, the triangulated plays (G1 principal media, G2 production bundle, J1 incubator) become significantly easier to close and fund.
Next Steps

To validate and progress

This document is an early-stage ideation brief. The following steps convert it into an actionable proposal.

1
Validate with Google AU counterparts

Confirm which programs align with existing Google AU SMB/Agency team mandates and available funding mechanisms (MDF, SPIF, beta program access). Identify internal sponsors on the Google side.

2
Confirm Adobe APAC internal sponsorship

Identify the Adobe APAC marketing and partnerships leads who would own this program. Validate whether any of these plays already exist inside Adobe's global partner framework to avoid duplication.

3
Identify relevant Publicis relationships

Confirm which Publicis APAC relationships can carry a tri-party conversation — and whether those individuals hold the seniority to make commercial commitments beyond the retained brief.

4
Build one-page business cases for entry points

Size the commercial opportunity for A1+A2 and C3. Estimate investment required, expected trial activations, and revenue attributable to each party. Use these as the pitch document for first conversations.

5
Agree scope boundary for Publicis retained vs. commercial

Before any program is proposed externally, align internally on which deliverables sit within the retained relationship and which require a separate arrangement. Prevents commercial ambiguity at the proposal stage.

6
Determine AU-first vs. APAC scale from the outset

Decide whether pilot programs are designed as AU-only proof-of-concepts or built with APAC replication in mind from the start. The latter requires more structural investment upfront but significantly improves the business case for Adobe and Google globally.
